APJ (Abdul Kalam Azad) refers to Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, often known as APJ Abdul Kalam. He was an Indian scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his role in India's nuclear and missile programs, he was also a prominent figure in the fields of aerospace and defense technology. He played a pivotal role in the development of India's satellite launch vehicles and the country's nuclear capabilities. Presidency: As the President of India, Dr. Kalam became one of the most popular and respected figures. His tenure was marked by his focus on youth empowerment, technological development, and his commitment to making India a global power in science and technology.
